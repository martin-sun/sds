How Redox Flow Batteries Work

  • Two external tanks with positively and negatively charged electrolytes
  • Pump system circulates electrolytes into the flow battery stack
  • Ion exchange membrane allows ions to pass but prevents electrolyte mixing
  • No salt bridge needed ‚Äî the membrane maintains charge balance efficiently
4 / 7

During Discharge

  • Negative electrode undergoes oxidation ‚Äî loses electrons
  • Electrons flow through external circuit creating electrical current
  • Positive electrode undergoes reduction ‚Äî accepts electrons
  • Ions move through membrane to maintain charge balance
5 / 7

During Recharge

  • External electricity reverses the entire process
  • Electrons pushed back in opposite direction
  • Positive electrode oxidizes, negative electrode reduces
  • Energy stored in chemical form for future use
  • Makes flow batteries rechargeable and sustainable
